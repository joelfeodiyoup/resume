These are my notes in preparation for an upcoming concert, Beat, Munich, June 19. I am very excited for this one. I like to listen to all the albums again leading up to it, to get my head in the right spot.

Beat is a reformation of the classic band King Crimson, playing albums from their classic "Beat" period made up of four albums.

The original line up was Robert Fripp, Adrian Belew, Tony Levin, Bill Bruford. For this lineup, Robert Fripp is replaced by Steve Vai, and Bill Bruford is replaced with Danny Carey, making the live lineup Adrian Belew, Steve Vai, Tony Levin and Danny Carey. This is really a supergroup.

### Beat - 1982

The first song reminded me immediately of Talking Heads. I thought `this album influenced talking heads`, but apparently it was the other way `talking heads influenced this album`, through guitarist Adrian Belew.

- 1980, Belew befriended Talking Heads, invited to perform with them live
- Belew meets King Crimson guitarist Robert Fripp at a Steve Reich concert.
- Belew is a session guitarist on Talking Heads 1980 album Remain In The Light https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Remain_in_Light
- Beat is release June 18 1982.

**songs**

- **Neal and Jack and Me**
  - apparently is about Beat Generation writers Neal Cassady and Jack Kerouac.
  - Wikipedia: Adrian Belew recalls being "prompted by a note saying 'I'm wheels, I am moving wheels'" given to him by Robert Fripp, who suggested that Beat writings become the "lyrical underpinning" of the album after he saw Belew reading a work by Kerouac.
- **Heartbeat**
- **Sartori in Tangier** - really nice synth sections
- **Waiting Man** - the tapping sounds very much like Joe Satriani's "midnight". Could have been influenced by it. Release 1987. (google says no. Influenced by Al di Meola and EVH)
- **Neurotica**
  - nice stream of conscience lyrics.
  - riff throwbacks to 'discipline' album
  - there's a "Beat Era" magazine named Neurotica. It was started as "a literary exposition, defense, and correlation of the problems and personalities that in our culture are defined as 'neurotic'. … We are interested in exploring the creativeness of this man who has been forced to live under-ground, and yet lights an utter darkness with his music, poetry, painting, and writing."
- **Two Hands**
- **The Howler**
  - “The Howler” refers to the Beat poem Howl by Allen Ginsberg, which Fripp suggested to Belew as inspiration for the lyrics
- **Requiem**
  - during recording, Fripp recorded over Belew's part. Belew told him to leave the studio. The band didn't reunite until the tour.
- **Absent Lovers** (bonus. Not on original recording)
